How to use this app
1
Set conditions on the Record tab — enter the True Wind Speed (TWS) and True Wind Direction (TWD). You can update these freely while recording and each data point will be stamped with the wind conditions at that moment.
2
Select your sail config — tap your mainsail reef, headsail, and any extras like spinnaker or Code 0.
3
Enable GPS then tap the orange Record button when you are settled on a steady heading and speed. Tap again to stop and save the session.
4
Repeat for many angles — sail upwind, reaching, and downwind legs in the same conditions to fill in the polar. The more sessions you record, the more complete your polars become.
5
Check the Polar and Table tabs to see your boat's performance envelope build up over time.
Polar Diagram — Reading the chart

The polar diagram is a semicircle. The boat sits at the centre top. Distance from the centre = boat speed in knots. Angle from centre = True Wind Angle (TWA), where 0° is directly into the wind and 180° is directly downwind.

Each coloured curve represents one True Wind Speed (TWS) band. A faster boat speed at a given angle pushes the curve further out from the centre.

Solid coloured curve — Average boat speed at that wind angle across all your recorded sessions. This is your typical, reliable performance.
Hollow diamond — Best speed ever recorded at that angle, when it is notably faster than your average. Shows your boat's ceiling on a good day.
Filled circle with white ring — Best VMG angle. This is the point of sail where you make the most progress directly upwind or downwind. Sail here when beating or running, not at 0° or 180°.
VMG — Velocity Made Good

VMG measures how fast you are actually moving toward your destination, as opposed to through the water at an angle. It is calculated as:

VMG = Boat Speed × cos(TWA)

Sailing directly into the wind gives zero boat speed, so VMG = 0. Sailing at 90° gives good speed but zero upwind progress. The best VMG angle is the sweet spot in between — typically 35°–50° upwind and 140°–165° downwind depending on your boat.

The Best VMG Angles card on the Polar tab tells you the exact angle and VMG speed for each wind strength. Use these numbers to decide when to tack or gybe for maximum performance.

Table Tab

The table shows average boat speed (AVG) and VMG for every combination of True Wind Angle (rows) and True Wind Speed (columns). The fastest speed in each row is highlighted in green.

The bottom two rows show the best upwind VMG angle (green) and best downwind VMG angle (amber) for each wind strength — these are your target angles for racing or efficient passagemaking.

Tap Export CSV to save the full table to a spreadsheet.

Key Terms
TWSTrue Wind Speed — wind speed relative to the water, not the boat.
TWATrue Wind Angle — angle between your heading and the true wind. 0° = head to wind, 180° = dead downwind.
TWDTrue Wind Direction — compass direction the wind is coming from.
SOGSpeed Over Ground — your actual speed measured by GPS.
COGCourse Over Ground — your actual heading measured by GPS.
VMGVelocity Made Good — your speed of progress directly toward (upwind) or away from (downwind) the wind source.
PolarA chart showing your boat's speed at every wind angle and wind speed — the complete performance map of the boat.
